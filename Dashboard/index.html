Chapter 01 · The Flip Story

163 Seats Changed Hands.
One Party Collected Most of Them.

In 2021, Tamil Nadu had a two-party battle between DMK and AIADMK. By 2026, a new entrant — TVK — had become the single largest party with 108 seats, making it a three-cornered contest. Of the 234 constituencies, 163 switched their winning party. That is a 70% churn rate — one of the highest in recent Tamil Nadu history.

Chapter 02 · The Vote Share Story

TVK Matched DMK's 2021 Vote Share
Without Cannibalising It.

The central question of 2026: where did TVK's 35.1% state-wide vote share come from? Did it pull from DMK, from AIADMK, or from voters who sat out 2021? Region by region, TVK's 2026 numbers mirror DMK's 2021 numbers with striking precision — while both established parties contracted simultaneously.

Where Did The Extra Votes Come From?

Voter turnout rose from 73.4% in 2021 to 86.2% in 2026 — nearly 13 percentage points and a state record. Millions of votes entered the pool that were simply not there five years ago. TVK's arrival may have mobilised previously non-voting citizens, expanding the electorate rather than only redistributing it.

Chapter 03 · The Margin of Victory Story

Victories Got Slimmer.
Tamil Nadu Became a
Three-Horse Race.

In 2021, with a two-party contest, winners typically captured 45–50% of votes. In 2026, with TVK, DMK, and AIADMK splitting the electorate, winning became far harder. Average margins fell, outright majorities became rare, and 61 constituencies were decided with the winner holding fewer than 35% of valid votes.

The Story in Three Acts

Tamil Nadu 2026:
A State Remapped.

Three data stories, one unified narrative. Each chapter answers the question the previous one raises — and together they explain not just who won, but what kind of election Tamil Nadu just had.

1
The Flip (Ch. 1)

163 of 234 constituencies changed their winning party. TVK collected 108 seats, with 65 coming directly from constituencies DMK held in 2021. A 70% churn rate — the map was almost entirely redrawn.

2
The Vote (Ch. 2)

TVK's 35.1% state-wide share mirrored DMK's 38% in 2021 — region by region, the lines are near-identical. A record 86.2% turnout suggests new voters entered the pool alongside any transfers.

3
The Margin (Ch. 3)

The three-way split compressed victories sharply. Average margin fell 27%. Seats won with <35% jumped from 2 to 61. The map looks decisive in seats — the margins reveal something more fragile.
